Shreemad Bhagvad Geeta

Chapter 1 of the Bhagavad Gita, titled "Arjuna Vishada Yoga" or "The Yoga of Arjuna's Dejection," consists of 46 verses in total. These verses set the stage for the dialogue between Lord Krishna and Arjuna, highlighting Arjuna's moral dilemma and emotional turmoil on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. It serves as a prelude to the profound teachings that follow in the subsequent chapters of the Bhagavad Gita.

Chatper 1, Verse 1

धृतराष्ट्र उवाच | धर्मक्षेत्रे कुरुक्षेत्रे समवेता युयुत्सवः | मामकाः पाण्डवाश्चैव किमकुर्वत संजय ||

Dhritarashtra uvacha: Dharmakshetre Kurukshetre samaveta yuyutsavah, Mamakah Pandavaschaiva kimakurvata Sanjaya.

Translation: Dhritarashtra said: O Sanjaya, what did my sons and the sons of Pandu do when they had gathered together, eager for battle, on the holy plain of Kurukshetra?

This verse captures the inquiry of King Dhritarashtra, seeking to learn from his charioteer, Sanjaya, about the actions and intentions of his own sons (the Kauravas) and the sons of Pandu (the Pandavas) on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. It sets the stage for the ensuing dialogue and teachings that unfold throughout the Bhagavad Gita.
